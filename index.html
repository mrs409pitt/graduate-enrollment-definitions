Graduate Recruitment & Enrollment Strategy Framework

The graduate enrollment lifecycle can be organized into four strategic phases, each with distinct objectives and stakeholder responsibilities. Understanding these phases helps align team efforts and optimize resource allocation.

1

Recruitment & Marketing

Stages Covered:

Awareness ‚Üí Consider

Area of Focus

Student Focus:

  • Student Mindset: "I need a graduate degree for my career goals" / "What programs exist in my field?"
  • Student Actions: Searching online, clicking ads, visiting websites, attending fairs

Staff Focus:

  • Develop and execute brand awareness campaigns
  • Manage digital advertising
  • Attend fairs and events
  • Create content
  • Drive web traffic (SEO/SEM)

Best Practices

Primary Tactics:

  • Digital advertising (Google, LinkedIn, social media)
  • Search engine optimization (SEO)
  • Content marketing (blogs, program pages)
  • Graduate school fairs and events
  • Partner organizations and employers

Success Indicators:

  • Website traffic volume
  • Social media reach and engagement
  • Event attendance
  • Brand awareness metrics

Primary Roles: Marketing Team, Recruitment Officers

2

Enrollment Nurturing

Stages Covered:

Engage ‚Üí Decision

Area of Focus

Student Focus:

  • Student Mindset: "I want to learn more about this specific program" / "How does this fit my career goals?"
  • Student Actions: Downloading materials, attending webinars, requesting counselor meetings, exploring curriculum

Staff Focus:

  • Follow up with inquiries
  • Provide program information
  • Answer questions
  • Nurture relationships
  • Encourage applications

Best Practices

Primary Tactics:

  • Personalized email campaigns
  • One-on-one counselor consultations
  • Information sessions and webinars
  • Virtual campus tours
  • Student and alumni testimonials
  • Faculty connections

Success Indicators:

  • Inquiry volume and rate
  • Email engagement rates
  • Webinar attendance
  • Inquiry-to-application conversion

Primary Roles: Recruitment Officers, Admissions Counselors

3

Admissions

Stages Covered:

Applying ‚Üí Admissions ‚Üí Decision/Offer

Area of Focus

Student Focus:

  • Student Mindset: "I'm ready to apply" / "What materials do I need?" / "Was I admitted?"
  • Student Actions: Starting applications, gathering materials (transcripts, recommendations), submitting applications, waiting for decisions

Staff Focus:

  • Assist with application submission
  • Provide technical support
  • Evaluate applications
  • Conduct interviews (if applicable)
  • Make admission decisions
  • Assemble financial aid packages

Best Practices

Primary Tactics:

  • Application portal guidance
  • Progress reminders and nudges
  • Requirements checklists
  • Deadline reminders
  • Decision letters with clear next steps
  • Personalized financial aid packages

Success Indicators:

  • Application starts and submission volume
  • Application completion rate
  • Decision turnaround time
  • Admit rate

Primary Roles: Admissions Counselors, Application Processors, Admissions Committee, Financial Aid Officers

4

Yield & Melt Prevention

Stages Covered:

Accepts/Deposits ‚Üí Enroll

Area of Focus

Student Focus:

  • Student Mindset: "Should I accept this offer?" / "What do I need to do to complete enrollment?" / "I'm excited but nervous"
  • Student Actions: Comparing offers, accepting admission, submitting deposit, completing enrollment forms, registering for orientation and classes

Staff Focus:

  • Yield communications
  • Facilitate enrollment completion
  • Coordinate orientation
  • Assist with course selection and registration
  • Remove barriers to enrollment
  • Prevent "summer melt"

Best Practices

Primary Tactics:

  • Admitted student webinars/events
  • Faculty and student connections
  • Campus visit days
  • Clear enrollment checklists
  • Orientation scheduling
  • Welcome communications
  • Pre-orientation programs

Success Indicators:

  • Offer-to-deposit yield rate
  • Deposit-to-enrollment rate (melt rate)
  • Enrollment completion timeline
  • Orientation attendance

Primary Roles: Enrollment Managers, Enrollment Coordinators, Academic Advisors, Registrar

üéØ Strategic Alignment

These four phases represent the complete enrollment lifecycle. Each phase requires different strategies, metrics, and team collaboration. Understanding which phase a prospect is in helps you deliver the right message at the right time with the appropriate level of support.

Phase Transitions: Critical Handoff Points

Phase 1 ‚Üí 2

Consider ‚Üí Engage: When a prospect submits an RFI, they transition from marketing to active recruitment nurturing. This is where personalized communication begins.

Phase 2 ‚Üí 3

Decision ‚Üí Applying: When a prospect starts an application, they move from enrollment nurturing to admissions processing. Application support becomes critical.

Phase 3 ‚Üí 4

Decision/Offer ‚Üí Accepts: When an admitted student deposits, they transition from admissions to yield management and enrollment services. Preventing melt is now the priority.

The Graduate Enrollment Funnel

The graduate enrollment funnel represents the complete journey from first awareness through matriculation. Understanding each stage and the conversion points between them is essential for managing enrollment effectively.

1
Awareness

Prospective students first become aware of your institution and graduate programs.

2
Consider

Prospects actively research your programs and seek detailed information.

3
Engage

Deeper exploration of specific programs and building relationships.

4
Decision

Serious evaluation of your programs against alternatives.

5
Applying

Actively working on or submitting applications.

6
Admissions

Applications under review and admission decisions being made.

7
Decision/Offer

Admitted students evaluating offers and making final decisions.

8
Accepts/Deposits

Students accept offers and submit enrollment deposits.

9
Enroll

Complete enrollment requirements and officially matriculate.

üí° Why Small Improvements = Big Results

Improving inquiry‚Üíapplication from 15% to 17% (just 2 percentage points) yields 20 additional applicants per 1,000 inquiries. Across 10 programs, that's potentially 10 more enrolled students - significant revenue impact!

6 Cross-Funnel KPIs That Matter Most

These six metrics span multiple funnel stages and provide the most comprehensive view of enrollment performance.

1. Funnel Conversion Rate

The percentage of prospects who advance from one funnel stage to the next.

(Number Advanced √∑ Number in Stage) √ó 100

Benchmarks: Inquiry‚ÜíApplication: 13-18% | App Completion: 50-60% | Offer‚ÜíDeposit: 60-70%

2. Days Between Stages

The average number of days for a prospect to move from one stage to the next (Pipeline Velocity).

Average (Date Next Stage - Date Current Stage)

Targets: Inquiry‚ÜíApp: ‚â§30 days | App‚ÜíDecision: <14 days

3. Cost per Stage Transition

Total marketing and recruitment expenditure required to move one student from one stage to the next.

Total Stage Spend √∑ Number of Transitions

Benchmarks: Cost/Inquiry: $140 avg | Cost/Enrolled: $2,850 avg

4. LTV:CAC Ratio

The ratio of Lifetime Value (total revenue from a student) to Customer Acquisition Cost.

Lifetime Value √∑ Customer Acquisition Cost

Targets: Minimum: 3:1 | Healthy: 5:1 | Excellent: 7:1+

5. Lead Quality by Channel

How effectively each marketing channel generates leads that convert deeper into the funnel and ultimately enroll.

(Conversion Rate √ó Volume) √∑ Cost

Track: Inquiry volume, conversion rates, cost per enrolled by channel

6. Source-to-Enrollment Attribution

Full-funnel tracking connecting each enrolled student back to their original marketing source and all touchpoints.

UTM tracking + multi-touch attribution

Models: First-touch, Last-touch, Multi-touch, Time-decay

Complete A-Z Terminology

Master this terminology to speak the language of enrollment management fluently. Click any term throughout this guide to jump to its definition.

A/B Testing

A method of comparing two versions of a marketing element (subject line, email content, landing page, call-to-action) to determine which performs better.

Acceptance Rate (Admit Rate)

The percentage of applicants offered admission to a program.

(Number Admitted √∑ Total Applicants) √ó 100

Activity (Salesforce)

A recordable action in Salesforce related to a lead, contact, or opportunity, such as an email sent, phone call made, meeting held, or task completed.

Admit

An applicant who has been offered admission to a program. Also called "admitted student" or "accepted student."

Affiliation (Salesforce)

A Salesforce record that creates a connection or relationship between a contact (person) and an account (school, department, or organization).

Applicant

A person who has submitted an application for admission to a graduate program.

Application Completion Rate

The percentage of started applications that are successfully submitted.

(Completed Applications √∑ Started Applications) √ó 100

Benchmark: 50-60%

Application Deadline

The date by which a completed application must be submitted for consideration. Graduate programs may have multiple deadlines (priority, regular, rolling).

Attrition Rate

The percentage of students who leave a program before completing their degree.

(Students Who Left √∑ Initial Cohort) √ó 100

Audience Builder (Marketing Cloud)

A Marketing Cloud tool used to create, segment, and target specific groups of prospective or current students based on defined criteria.

Automation Studio (Marketing Cloud)

A Marketing Cloud feature for automating repetitive marketing tasks and processes, such as sending scheduled emails, importing data, or updating contact lists.

Best Practices

Proven strategies, tactics, and approaches that consistently produce superior results in recruitment and enrollment management.

CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)

The total cost to enroll one student, including all marketing, recruitment, and related expenses.

Total Marketing & Recruitment Spend √∑ Number of Enrolled Students

Graduate Avg: $2,850

Campaign (Marketing Cloud)

A coordinated marketing initiative in Marketing Cloud, typically involving planning, execution, and tracking of multiple activities (emails, social media, ads) directed at a target audience to achieve a specific goal.

Campaign (Salesforce)

A Salesforce feature used to plan, manage, track, and analyze marketing efforts and communications directed at specific audiences in the recruitment pipeline.

Case (Salesforce)

A Salesforce record representing a student question, issue, concern, or feedback that needs to be tracked, managed, and resolved.

Click-Through Rate (CTR)

The percentage of email recipients who click on one or more links in an email.

(Clicks √∑ Delivered Emails) √ó 100

Benchmark: 2.5-3.5%

Contact (Salesforce)

A Salesforce record representing an individual person, typically a prospective student, applicant, current student, or alumnus.

Contact Builder (Marketing Cloud)

A Marketing Cloud feature for managing, organizing, and viewing all contact data, including attributes, relationships, and preferences.

Content Builder (Marketing Cloud)

A drag-and-drop editor in Marketing Cloud for creating, storing, and managing marketing content (emails, landing pages, images, text blocks).

Conversion Rate

The percentage of individuals who complete a desired action or advance from one stage to the next in the enrollment funnel.

(Number Who Converted √∑ Total Number in Stage) √ó 100

Cost per Enrolled Student (CPE)

Same as Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC). The total cost to recruit and enroll one student.

Total Marketing & Recruitment Spend √∑ Number of Enrolled Students

Cost per Lead (CPL)

The average cost to generate one inquiry or lead.

Marketing Spend √∑ Number of Inquiries

Average: $140

Cost per Stage Transition

The cost to move one prospect from one stage of the funnel to the next.

Stage Marketing Spend √∑ Number Who Advanced to Next Stage

CRM (Customer Relationship Management)

A technology platform for managing all interactions and relationships with current and prospective students throughout their journey. At Pitt, Salesforce serves as the primary CRM.

Dashboard (Salesforce)

A visual display of key data points, metrics, and charts created from Salesforce reports, used to monitor recruitment and enrollment performance at a glance.

Data Extensions (Marketing Cloud)

Tables within Marketing Cloud that store data, such as student/contact information, used for segmentation, personalization, and journey orchestration.

Days Between Stages (Pipeline Velocity)

The average number of days it takes for a prospect to move from one funnel stage to the next.

Average (Date Entered Next Stage - Date Entered Current Stage)

Deferred Admission

When an admitted student requests to postpone enrollment to a future term. The student's admission offer remains valid for the deferred term.

Demonstrated Interest

Actions showing genuine interest in a program, such as campus visits, event attendance, engagement with communications, and interactions with admissions staff.

Deposit to Enrollment Rate

The percentage of students who deposit who actually matriculate and begin classes. Also known as "melt rate" when calculating the inverse.

(Number Enrolled √∑ Number Deposited) √ó 100

Benchmark: 95%+

Dynamic Content (Marketing Cloud)

Email or web content that automatically changes based on the recipient's specific attributes, behaviors, or preferences, enabling personalized communication at scale.

Early Action

An application option where students apply by an early deadline and receive an admission decision earlier than regular decision applicants, but are not required to commit.

Early Decision

A binding application option where students apply early and, if admitted, must enroll. Less common in graduate programs than undergraduate.

Email Open Rate

The percentage of delivered emails that are opened by recipients.

(Opens √∑ Delivered Emails) √ó 100

Higher Ed Avg: 44%

Email Studio (Marketing Cloud)

The Marketing Cloud component dedicated to creating, sending, tracking, and analyzing email campaigns and communications.

Enrollment

The process where admitted students officially register and commit to attending. Also refers to the final count of students who begin classes.

Event Attendance Rate

The percentage of registrants who actually attend an event.

(Attendees √∑ Registrants) √ó 100

Financial Aid

Funding provided to students to help pay for graduate education, including scholarships, assistantships, fellowships, grants, and loans.

First-Year Retention

The percentage of students who return for their second year of study.

(Students Returning for Year 2 √∑ Initial First-Year Cohort) √ó 100

Graduate Avg: ~75.9%

Funnel Conversion Rate

The percentage who successfully move from one stage of the enrollment funnel to the next. One of the 6 critical cross-funnel KPIs.

Graduation Rate

The percentage of students who complete their degree within a specified timeframe (typically 6 years for master's programs).

Master's Avg: ~64.2%

IERs (Individual Email Results)

Records written into Salesforce documenting emails sent through Marketing Cloud to contacts or leads, including delivery and engagement metrics.

Inquiry-to-Application Rate

The percentage of inquiries who submit an application.

(Applicants √∑ Inquiries) √ó 100

Benchmark: 13-18%

Integration (Salesforce)

The technical process of connecting Salesforce with other systems or platforms to enable data sharing and synchronized workflows. Key integration: Salesforce ‚Üî Marketing Cloud (data syncs every 15 minutes).

Interaction (Salesforce)

A Salesforce record type where initial data is captured before it's processed and assigned to a Contact, Lead, or Opportunity.

Journey (Marketing Cloud)

An automated, personalized series of communications and touchpoints orchestrated through Marketing Cloud Journey Builder, designed to guide individuals through a specific experience based on their behaviors, attributes, and interactions.

Journey Builder (Marketing Cloud)

A Marketing Cloud tool for designing, automating, and managing complex, multi-step, personalized marketing journeys that guide contacts through experiences based on their actions and attributes.

Lead (Salesforce)

A Salesforce record representing a potential student who has expressed interest but has not yet been qualified or converted into a Contact with an Opportunity. When qualified, Lead converts to Contact + Opportunity.

Lead Quality by Channel

A measure of how effectively each marketing channel generates leads that convert deeper into the funnel and ultimately enroll. One of the 6 critical cross-funnel KPIs.

LTV:CAC Ratio

The ratio of Lifetime Value (total revenue from a student) to Customer Acquisition Cost (total cost to recruit and enroll them). One of the 6 critical cross-funnel KPIs.

Lifetime Value √∑ Customer Acquisition Cost

Targets: Minimum: 3:1 | Healthy: 5:1 | Excellent: 7:1+

Marketing Cloud

Salesforce's digital marketing platform that enables personalized, automated, multi-channel marketing communications and customer journey management. Integrates with Salesforce CRM; data syncs every 15 minutes.

Matriculation

The formal process of entering as a student and beginning classes. An enrolled student who has matriculated.

Offer to Deposit Yield

The percentage of admitted students who submit an enrollment deposit.

(Number Deposited √∑ Number Admitted) √ó 100

Benchmark: 60-70%

Opportunity (Salesforce)

A Salesforce record used to track the recruitment and application pipeline from a prospective student through to matriculation. Typically one opportunity per program application (student may have multiple Opportunities for different programs).

Opportunity Stages (Salesforce)

The stages an opportunity moves through in Salesforce, typically: Prospective ‚Üí In-Progress Application ‚Üí Application ‚Üí Admit ‚Üí Deposit ‚Üí Enrolled.

Pipeline Velocity

The speed at which prospects move through funnel stages. Measured as Days Between Stages. One of the 6 critical cross-funnel KPIs.

Predictive Intelligence (Einstein Intelligence)

Marketing Cloud's AI-powered tool that uses data analytics and machine learning to predict student behaviors, optimize send times, recommend content, and improve recruitment outcomes.

Prospect

A potential student in the early awareness stage who has not yet submitted an inquiry or expressed formal interest.

Report (Salesforce)

A structured query and display of Salesforce data, used to analyze recruitment and enrollment performance.

Response Time

The time it takes for staff to respond to a prospect inquiry or question. Target: <24 hours (ideal: <4 hours).

RFI (Request for Information)

A form submitted by a prospective student to request more information about a program. Creates an inquiry record in the CRM.

Rolling Admission

An admission policy where applications are reviewed as they are received, and decisions are made on an ongoing basis rather than on specific decision dates.

Salesforce

A cloud-based Customer Relationship Management (CRM) platform used to manage student data, track interactions, manage recruitment pipelines, and execute marketing campaigns. The primary CRM at the University of Pittsburgh.

Scholarship

Financial aid awarded to students based on merit, need, or other criteria. Does not need to be repaid.

Source-to-Enrollment Attribution

Full-funnel tracking that connects each enrolled student back to their original marketing source and tracks all touchpoints along their journey. One of the 6 critical cross-funnel KPIs.

Stealth Applicant

A student who applies without previously submitting an inquiry or engaging in the typical recruitment process.

Subscribers (Marketing Cloud)

Individuals in Marketing Cloud who have opted in to receive communications through email, SMS, or other channels.

Transcript

An official record of a student's academic coursework and grades, required as part of most graduate applications.

True Conversion Rate

A conversion rate calculated based on qualified prospects only, excluding unqualified inquiries.

Turnaround Time

The time between when an action is initiated and when it's completed (e.g., from application submission to admission decision).

Waitlist

Qualified applicants who may be offered admission if spaces become available after initial offers have been made.

Website Sessions

The number of visits to your website within a given timeframe. A session ends after 30 minutes of inactivity or at midnight.

Win/Close Rate

Same as Overall Win Rate. The percentage of applications that result in enrollment.

(Number Enrolled √∑ Number of Applicants) √ó 100

Yield Rate

The percentage of admitted students who enroll.

(Number Enrolled √∑ Number Admitted) √ó 100

Benchmark: 60-70%

30-Day Quick Start Guide

New to enrollment metrics? Follow this plan to get up and running with data-driven enrollment management.

Week 1: Establish Baseline

Goal: Know where you stand today

  1. Pull current numbers for all 6 cross-funnel KPIs
  2. Document where each metric comes from
  3. Create simple dashboard or spreadsheet
  4. Share baseline with your team

Week 2: Set Targets

Goal: Define success

  1. Compare your baseline to benchmarks
  2. Set realistic 3-month improvement goals
  3. Assign ownership for each KPI
  4. Schedule weekly 30-minute metric review

Week 3: Identify Quick Wins

Goal: Get early momentum

  1. Find your biggest gap vs. benchmark
  2. Pick ONE tactical improvement to implement
  3. Launch the improvement and track impact
  4. Document what you learn

Week 4: Build Cadence

Goal: Make metrics routine

  1. Confirm weekly metric review is happening
  2. Create decision framework: "If X drops below Y, we do Z"
  3. Train team on using metrics for decisions
  4. Plan Month 2 improvements based on learnings
üí™ Remember: Start Small, Think Big

You don't need perfect data or perfect dashboards to start using metrics effectively. Pick ONE metric to improve this month. Track it weekly. Make decisions based on what you see. Learn. Adjust. Repeat.

Small improvements compound over time into significant results.
